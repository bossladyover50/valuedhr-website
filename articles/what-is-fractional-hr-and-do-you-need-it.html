What Is Fractional HR and Is It Right for Your Business?

By Michelle Mendez  •  June 1, 2026  •  5 min read
← Back to Blog

Fractional HR is a model where a senior HR professional works with your business on a part-time or project basis, providing the strategy, guidance, and oversight that a full-time HR director would — without the full-time cost.

The model has grown significantly in recent years, partly because more experienced HR professionals are offering their services this way, and partly because small business owners have recognized that they need senior HR capability earlier in their growth than they can justify a full-time hire.

What Fractional HR Actually Does

A fractional HR partner is not a recruiter and is not an HR coordinator. They are a strategic resource who can help you build your HR foundation, navigate complex people situations, ensure compliance, design and run your hiring process, manage employee relations issues, and develop your managers. They bring the judgment of someone who has seen these situations before — which is precisely what is missing in most small businesses.

What It Costs

Fractional HR engagements typically run between $1,500 and $6,000 per month depending on scope, experience level, and hours. That compares to $85,000 to $130,000 per year for a full-time HR manager — fully loaded. For most businesses under 75 employees, the math strongly favors fractional.

When It Is the Right Fit

Fractional HR works well when you need senior HR judgment more than you need high-volume HR administration. If you are processing payroll, managing complex benefits administration, or onboarding dozens of employees per month, you may need both fractional HR leadership and some level of HR administrative support. The two are not mutually exclusive.

How to Evaluate a Fractional HR Partner

Look for someone with experience in your industry and company size, a track record of building HR foundations rather than just maintaining existing systems, the ability to communicate complex HR and legal concepts in plain language, and a clear scope of what is and is not included in the engagement.

The best time to engage a fractional HR partner is before you have an HR crisis. By the time a complaint is filed or a key person resigns, you are in reactive mode. The foundation-building work happens best when there is nothing on fire.
